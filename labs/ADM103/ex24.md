# Exercise 24 — Role Distribution & Verification

> [!NOTE]
> Rewritten for A4H (ABAP 2023 Docker)

**Scenario**

After creating a role in the child client and assigning it centrally via the CUA, you will now trigger distribution manually, verify that the role has arrived in the child, and confirm that a newly assigned user inherits the correct authorizations.

Prerequisites:
- **Central system:** A4H client 001
- **Child system:** A4H client 000
- **Role created in Exercise 23:** `Z_ENDUSER_ROLE`
- **User assigned in Exercise 23:** `Z_TRAIN_USER`

## Task 1: Trigger manual role distribution from the CUA

1. Log on to the **central system** (A4H client 001) as `TRAIN-01`.
2. Call transaction `SCUM`.
3. Select **Tools → Distribution → Distribute Roles**.
4. In the selection screen, enter `Z_ENDUSER_ROLE` in the **Role** field.
5. Leave **Target system** blank to distribute to all connected child systems.
6. Choose **Execute** (F8).
7. Confirm the distribution job has been triggered — you should see a success message with the number of target systems.
8. Note the batch job name for the next task.

## Task 2: Monitor the distribution job

1. Call transaction `SM37`.
2. In **Job Name**, enter `CUA*`.
3. Set **From date** to today and **Status** to `Finished` and `Active`.
4. Choose **Execute**.
5. Locate your distribution job and check its status.
6. Open the job log and confirm: **Role distributed successfully** appears for child system `A4H-000`.

## Task 3: Verify role arrival in the child client

1. Switch to the **child client** (A4H client 000). Log on as `TRAIN-01`.
2. Call transaction `PFCG`.
3. In the **Role** field, enter `Z_ENDUSER_ROLE` and press Enter.
4. Confirm the role exists and its status shows **Distributed** (the lock icon indicates it is read-only in the child).
5. Open the **Authorizations** tab and verify the authorization objects match what was defined in the central system.

## Task 4: Validate user authorizations

1. Still in the child client (000), call transaction `SU01`.
2. Enter user `Z_TRAIN_USER` and choose **Display**.
3. Navigate to the **Roles** tab — confirm `Z_ENDUSER_ROLE` is listed.
4. Navigate to the **Profiles** tab — confirm the corresponding profile `Z_ENDUSER_ROLE` is present.
5. Call transaction `SU53` while logged in as `Z_TRAIN_USER` (in a separate session) to verify no authorization failures for the objects granted by the role.
